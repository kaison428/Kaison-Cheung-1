Transport I Position Paper: Autonomous Vehicle & Traffic Congestion

At the beginning of my Transport I course in the third year, we had to write a position paper regarding some of the most controversial topics currently in transportation engineering. I chose to write about the impact of autonomous vehicles on traffic congestion because I wanted to learn how machine learning and automation will impact our city. With that, we can also understand how transportation engineers and urban planners can adapt to the emergence of new technology and create a more connected and accessible city.

After my research, I found out that AVs will not improve traffic congestion for two reasons. First, a small fraction of conventional vehicles could significantly reduce traffic flow. However, it is unlikely that conventional vehicles will phase out entirely soon as people enjoy the driving experience. Second, the traffic volume could increase as people prefer owning additional AVs to earn passive income and more AVs could be cruising on the streets to avoid paying for parking.
