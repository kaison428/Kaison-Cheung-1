Engineering Economics and Decision Making: Ontario Power Generation (OPG) case study

The OPG case study is a team project for the Engineering Economics and Decision-Making course. This course provides an overview of engineering economics and fundamental concepts, such as the time value of money and inflation, which allow us to evaluate various projects and make fair decisions systematically.

In this case study, we evaluated the economic value and the social impact of the two project options – natural gas plant and wind power plant – by comparing multiple economic indicators (e.g. present worth, annual worth and internal rate of return) and performing various analyses to account for the social and environmental impacts (e.g. cost-effective analysis and benefit-cost analysis).

Our team developed an Excel model to calculate the cash flow and conduct sensitivity analyses for both projects. My responsibilities were to research and compute the amount of carbon tax applied to the natural gas plant project, determine how varying construction costs impact both options and the break-even electricity prices. Data table, forecast and other data analysis functions were used extensively to effectively perform the tasks mentioned.
